USINT TOO Point of Contact


The current USINT TOO Point of Contact is indicated by Lime color.

Shifts run midnight plus ε on start day to midnight minus ε on last day.

The TOO POC becomes the responsible scientist for all bare ACIS TOO/DDTs APPROVED DURING their on-duty period. Grating and HRC observations are assigned to instrument specialists as are solar system and Crab observations, with the exception of cases where a grating is used primarily to reduce the source event rate (see below) and the observer has minimal interest in the dispersed spectrum. Those not-really-grating observations will remain under the management of the TOO POC and not the instrument specialist.

Inserting the LETG reduces the source rate by a factor of ~8 at wavelengths longer than ~8 Ang (0th order vs no-grating), and by a factor of 3-10 for the HETG; the higher value is for a very soft source (E<2 keV) and the lower value for hard sources (E>4 keV). Use PIMMS for more detailed estimates.
